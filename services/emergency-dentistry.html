Common Questions

Emergency Dentistry FAQ

What counts as a dental emergency?
Severe toothache, broken or cracked tooth, knocked-out tooth, lost crown or filling, abscess or swelling, and dental trauma from an injury all require same-day care. When in doubt, call us.
What should I do if a tooth is knocked out?
Keep it moist — place it in milk or hold it between your cheek and gum. Call us immediately. Reimplantation is most successful within 30–60 minutes.
Do you offer sedation for emergency visits?
Yes. If you're in pain and anxious, we offer oral conscious sedation and nitrous oxide to make your emergency visit as comfortable as possible.
What if my emergency happens after hours?
Call our main number (818) 706-6077 for after-hours guidance. We do our best to accommodate true emergencies as soon as possible.
How quickly can I get seen?
We prioritize emergency cases and typically see patients the same day they call. Call as early as possible for the best chance of a morning appointment.
Will my insurance cover emergency treatment?
Most PPO dental plans cover emergency exams and many emergency procedures. Our team verifies your coverage at check-in and discusses any costs upfront.
